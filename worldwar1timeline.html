Prior to the war, Germany has an alliance with Austro-Hungarian's and Italy. This was known as triple alliance. August 3, 1914: Germany declares war on France

As France had an alliance with Russia, the Germans had developed a plan known as Schliffen plan which was to come into action if they went to war with both France and Russia.

So, acting according to this plan they start sending troops to the border of France. But to do this they had to cross Belgium. But, Belgium had signed "Treaty of London" way back in 1839, which meant that the British had to protect the Belgians in a state of war.

So, the British give Germans an ultimatum to withdraw from Belgium.

May 7, 1915: Lusitania sunk

British ocean liner Lusitania is torpedoed by a German U-boat off the southern coast of Ireland. It sinks in just 18 minutes, and nearly 1,200 people are killed, including 128 U.S. citizens.

This act sparks international outrage and contributes to the United States joining the war against Germany.

February 21, 1916: Battle Of Verdun

The Germans mounted an attack on the French at Verdun.

Over the next 9 months, the French and German armies at Verdun, France, suffer over 700,000 casualties, including some 300,000 killed.

Although the fighting continued for nine months, the battle was inconclusive.

May 31, 1916: Battle of Jutland begins.

The largest naval battle of the war, the Battle of Jutland, is fought between Britain and Germany in the North Sea.

In the two-day battle, the British lose 111,980 tons of shipping with 6,945 casualties. The Germans lose 62,233 tons of shipping with 2,921 casualties. Each side claims victory.

July 1, 1916: The First Battle of the Somme begins

In an effort to draw German troops away from Verdun, the Allies mount a major offensive that opens with a large-scale artillery barrage that lasts five days.

By the time the Somme campaign ground to a halt some four and a half months later, the combined casualties of both sides topped 1,000,000.

January 19, 1917 - The British intercept the Zimmerman Telegram.

Germany tries to convince Mexico to join the war in the telegram. This will result in the United States declaring war on Germany.

In the decoded message, German Foreign Minister Arthur Zimmermann promises the return of Texas, New Mexico and Arizona to Mexico as reward for siding with Germany if the U.S. enters the war.

Tsar Nicholas II abdicates the throne after a week of riots in the Russian capital of St. Petersburg. The Russian Revolution saw the overthrow of the Romanov dynasty and, ultimately, the rise to power of Vladimir Lenin and the Bolsheviks.

April 6 ,1917: The United States enters the war, declaring war on Germany.

In his address to Congress four days earlier, U.S. Pres. Woodrow Wilson had cited Germany’s practice of unrestricted submarine warfare and the “Zimmermann Telegram” as key reasons behind the abandonment of his long-standing policy of neutrality.

The day after an overwhelming majority in the Senate votes for war, President Wilson signs the declaration. The United States quickly puts the entire country on the road to war.
